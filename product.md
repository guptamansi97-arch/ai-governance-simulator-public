# AI Governance Stress Test Simulator

## Overview
The **AI Governance Stress Test Simulator** is an enterprise-grade strategic tool designed to evaluate the robustness and safety of AI initiatives before deployment. Using a multi-agent, multi-round simulation framework, the system subjects proposed AI projects to diverse professional perspectives and realistic operational "shocks."

## Core Philosophy: The Red-Teaming of Governance
Unlike static checklists, this simulator models dynamic organizational tension. It provides leadership with a high-fidelity "stress test" by simulating a debate between diverse professional stakeholders defined in a central registry.

The system dynamically adapts to any number of registered agents, such as:
- **CFO**: Focuses on capital preservation and ROI clarity.
- **Compliance**: Focuses on regulatory adherence and data integrity.
- **Operations**: Focuses on implementation feasibility and stability.
- **AI Evangelist**: Focuses on strategic upside and innovation potential.
- **Additional Stakeholders**: Easily add Legal, Security, or specialized SME agents.

## Simulation Structure

### Round 1: Independent Baseline (Bulk)
All agents perform an initial, "blind" evaluation of the initiative simultaneously. This establishes a baseline of independent professional bias.

### Round 2: Risk-Agenda-Driven Debate (Sequential)
Agents enter a structured debate where the agenda is pre-planned based on Round 1 risks. Each segment is led by a pre-assigned "owner" and challenged by domain-relevant "interjectors", ensuring a non-repetitive and high-friction consensus-building process.

### Round 3: Shock Injection (Bulk)
A significant operational constraint (shock) is applied based on initiative tags and industry relevance. All agents reassess the initiative’s resilience, exposing hidden vulnerabilities.

### Governance simulation engine
Orchestrates a multi-round stress test:
1.  **Baseline Evaluation**: Independent risk assessments.
2.  **Executive Debate**: High-friction cross-functional discussion.
3.  **Debate Analysis**: Structured extraction of stances, decisions, and coalitions.
4.  **Baseline Decision**: Committee consensus derived post-debate.
5.  **Shock Injection**: Resilience testing against tag-matched external stressors.
6.  **Analytics**: Weighted scoring using a three-stage decision model to calculate the **Post-Stress Decision**.

- **Strategic Signals**: Dual decision monitoring ("Baseline" vs "Post-Stress"). Rejections are reserved for major risk polarization, while pilot recommendations act as professional mitigations.

## Strategic Metrics
The system translates complex semantic debates into quantifiable executive metrics:
- **Baseline vs. Post-Stress Stance**: Tracking decision stability across shocks.
- **Final Consensus**: Qualitative convergence level (Low, Moderate, High).
- **Alignment Score**: Quantitative measure (0-100) of risk level consensus.
- **Fragility Index**: Measures sensitivity to unexpected operational stress.

## Key Features
- **Executive Dashboard**: A premium, visually-rich UI for decision-making.
- **Agent Reasoning Logs**: Full transparency into the "thought process" of each stakeholder.
- **Actionable Mitigations**: Automatically generated checklists and rollout conditions.
